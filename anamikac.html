About me
I am Anamika Chhabra. I love to learn new things!

I have a Master's degree in Information Technology from Guru Gobind Singh Indraprastha University, New Delhi and am currently studying for a PhD in Computer Science and Engineering Department at Indian Institute of Technology Ropar under Dr. Sudarshan Iyengar. The area of my research is to understand the shift that has taken place in the way we build knowledge, which includes understanding the dynamics of popular crowdsourced knowledge building portals such as Wikipedia, StackOverflow and Quora. My work involves analyzing the various characteristics of the collaborative knowledge building process and predicting the amount of knowledge produced, given certain parameters.

Apart from my PhD, I am also working as the Principal Investigator of a DST Project being executed at IIT Ropar. This project was selected under the Women Scientist Scheme (WOS-A) of DST. The project is about providing a crowdsourced solution based on annotations to facilitate up-to-date and collaborative textbooks for secondary school students.

Before joining PhD, I taught for around 10 years in engineering colleges in Delhi and Chandigarh. I was SPOC of IBM Centre of Excellence at Indo Global College of Engineering, Chandigarh for three years. I was also coordinator of Campus Connect Program (An initiative to connect Industry and Academia by Infosys) at MAIT, New Delhi for four years.

Experience at Malgudi Labs
I am working on crowdsourced knowledge building (KB) and we recently observed the existence of ecosystem on KB portals, which triggers them to produce more knowledge. I clearly see Malgudi too having the same traits. It has people possessing a variety of skills. Programming, theory, discussion, articulation, presentation and writing are the skills that are an essential ingredient for any research lab. We can easily spot people in Malgudi possessing all these skills to some extent and specializing in some of them. Every Malgudian complements each other with their unique specialities, i.e. there is an ecosystem here too! Being a part of Malgudi, and hence, getting an exposure to a variety of research problems due to discussions on topics ranging from crowdsourcing to core-periphery structure in networks to multiparty computations to centrality measures and ranking with local information has really helped me broaden my horizon.
