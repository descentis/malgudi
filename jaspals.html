About me
Hi! I’m Jaspal, a final year computer science undergrad at the Indian Institute of Technology Ropar, India. While I received my kindergarten to class 11 schooling from OOEHS, Sharjah, U.A.E, I received my class 12 schooling from Doon International School, Mohali, India. As a B.Tech student at IIT Ropar, I have spent most of my time learning new subjects that interest me and doing research in collaboration with P.hD scholars here in my college. For the little spare time I have, I like listening to music, watching Anime, doing pranayama/yoga early morning, teaching mathematics and watching documentaries/discussions on science, religion, philosophy, distinguished personalities and anything I find interesting. After my B.Tech I wish to opt for higher studies in a field that has a rich theoretical componenet and some philosophical roots.

Research Interests
Multiparty Computations, Social Networks, Knowledge Building

Experience
Time spent on Malgudi activities has certainly been one of the most memorable moments for me, whether it were the awe-inspiring lectures by Professor Sudarshan, the heated brainstorming sessions and debates, the reading club activity or the paper acceptance parties! The best part of the research environment here is the great collaborative spirit that all members of the group share. For example, I have had the opportunity to work with various research scholars at Malgudi on diverse projects, ranging from analysis of popularity of online social networking platforms, modelling the dynamics of knowledge building, understanding the benefits of caste reservation system in India from a social capital perspective to designing secure multiparty computation protocols for efficient social network analysis in the distributed setting. All in all, at Malgudi labs we work on collaborative projects, read and criticize popular science books, teach each other anything new we learn and of course have ample amount of fun! :)
